I am submitting my manuscript, **"RSV G selection analyses support constraint of
the CX3C/cystine-noose core and diversification in mucin-like regions,"** for

**Summary.** The respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) attachment glycoprotein G is a
variable surface antigen, yet it carries a short central conserved region (CCR)
whose CX3C chemokine-mimic motif and cystine noose are of interest as vaccine and
antibody targets. Using a uniform, pre-specified molecular-evolution pipeline
(codon alignment, maximum-likelihood phylogeny, and site-wise selection analysis
with FEL and MEME), first validated on influenza H3N2 haemagglutinin as a positive
control, I tested whether diversifying selection is enriched in the CCR/CX3C motif.
It is not: in both RSV-A and RSV-B, positively selected sites fall in the mucin-like
C-terminal domain and ON1/unmappable sequence rather than the conserved core, and a
threshold-free per-codon analysis indicates the CCR is under stronger purifying
constraint than the flanks (significant in RSV-A; a concordant but non-significant
trend in RSV-B). Mapping the per-codon signal onto the experimental structure
(PDB 5WN9) is consistent with purifying selection over the functional motif. The
results support continued monitoring and experimental study of the CX3C/cystine-noose
core but do not by themselves establish vaccine or therapeutic efficacy.

**Fit for a student journal.** This is original student research that applies
established, peer-reviewed methods to a well-defined and biologically motivated
question. The design is transparent: the hypothesis and region definitions were
fixed before selected-site positions were inspected, a positive control validates
the pipeline, and the interpretation is deliberately conservative. The manuscript is
self-contained and readable by non-specialist scientific reviewers, with plain-language
explanations of the selection methods and the rationale for the influenza control.

**Reproducibility and transparency.** All analysis code, accession lists, archived
input sequences, alignments, trees, FEL/MEME outputs, summary tables, figures, and
SHA256 checksums are publicly available and reproducible from the archived input
files forward (GitHub: https://github.com/zahid-bio/rsv-g-selection-analysis). Code
is released under the MIT License and the manuscript/data under CC BY 4.0. I disclose
the analysis's limitations openly, including the limited power of the primary
site-count test, the RSV-A/RSV-B asymmetry, a minor cross-subtype mapping loss, the
partial structural coverage, and the fact that the original sequence-download query
and seed were not captured.
